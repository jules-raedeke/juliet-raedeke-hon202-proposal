Procedure


Stimuli will be presented first as questions in a survey, most likely online. Stimuli will then be presented by myself in the format of an interview, in which I ask the participant questions (so, I will be presenting vocally). The same goes for stimulus presented in focus groups; I will be prompting the group myself (vocally).

Participants will answer my questions, either online through the survey, or vocally/non-verbally (if applicable) in interviews/focus groups. Their answers will be my data.

Step-by-Step Protocol: Survey

  • Participant-directed; I will provide campuses and their students with my survey, they will answer it themselves, and then submit it back to me
  • Once I have received enough responses (a number like 200 would be ideal, but will depend on the efficacy of my survey distribution), I will turn to coding analysis

Survey sample questions:

  • Do you feel like learning online is harder or easier for you?
  • Are you satisfied with your school's response to the pandemic?
  • Do you feel like your tuition is a fair amount for the classes you are taking?

Step-by-Step Protocol: Interviews

  • Participants will meet me at a predetermined time and location that I will inform them of through email after they have signified interest in doing an interview
  • They will sign a consent form (that I will have created) concerning the study, before the interview can ensue
  • Participants will sit across from me as I conduct the interview for no more than 20 minutes
  • I will begin recording our conversation right before I ask the first question
  • Questions will continue to be asked after the participant indicates that they are finished answering the initial question asked
  • After the interview is over, I will send them away with information on how to keep up with my study and where to contact me if they have further questions

Interview sample questions:

  • Do you feel the pandemic and consequent shift toward online classes has positively or negatively impacted your view of college?
  • Do you feel like the amount you pay for tuition is justified based on the quality of the education you are getting?
  • Do you think your enrolled peers feel the same way as you? Why or why not?

Step-by-Step Protocol: Focus Groups

  • Participants will meet me at a predetermined time and location that I will inform them of through email after they have signified interest in participating in a focus group
  • They will sign a consent form (that I will have created) concerning the study before the focus group can ensue
  • Participants will sit facing each other in a circle, myself included, as I conduct the focus group for no more than 45 minutes
  • I will begin recording right before I ask the first question. I will ask the group 3-5 questions; after asking a question, I will let them talk amongst themselves until they indicate they are ready for the next question
  • After each focus group is over, I will send them on their way with information on how to keep up with my study and where to contact me if they have further questions

Focus group sample questions:

  • Do you think the pandemic has changed how potential and current students view college and a college education? Why or why not?
  • Do you think that the attitude toward the value of a college education will continue to change over time? Why or why not?
  • Do you think colleges need to adjust tuition in general? How would you feel about a college education being free? Do you think more or less people would go to college if that were the case?
  • Why are you getting a degree? Do you think students are getting a degree based on personal interest or career practicality?
