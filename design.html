Design


Variables and Manipulations

To manipulate my stimuli, I could potentially send out a second survey with questions pertaining more to the responses I received during the first round. I think this would expand upon my initial data in a productive and specific way, as I would get more nuanced data the second time around. I could also create another round of focus groups, but place participants who expressed similar opinions in the same groups. I think this could provide an interesting support/rebuttal dynamic between a group that agrees with my hypothesis and one that does not. Changing the way I am dressed or appear to the participants in focus groups and interviews could be another way to manipulate my design.

When presenting participants with stimulus sentences, I could vary my tone of voice or the perceived intent that I present the questions in. In interviews and focus groups, I could be more or less engaged with my participants to see if my speaking influences their thinking at all, which would be another interesting point of data on the topic.

Controls

If I am going to vary my intonation or the phrasing of questions to alter my stimuli, I will assure that it is consistent and follows a script that I will write. I will also assure that the survey is only being taken by my target demographic. For the interviews and focus groups, I will keep the setting and set-up consistent, as well as controlling the way I am dressed in these groups, to make sure I appear the way I intend to my participants.

I will rule out or control apparent effects through extensive coding and theme-building. By going through this coding and theme-building process, I would negate the opportunity for there to be an “obvious answer” from my collected data.
